To: Letters@nzherald.co.nz
Subject: political reporting
Sir.-
 
Once again this month, I must remonstrate with you about the abysmal quality of your political reporting. John Armstrong ("Poll punctures United's status") writes that "the bubble appears to be bursting for United Future". And this on the basis of just one poll?! This is credible political reporting? I think not.
 
Your caption writer gets worse with the caption "FADING FORCE: Peter Dunne's support is sliding fast." And he/she knows this for a fact after just one poll!!?? A poll with a margin of error of 3.2 percent, meaning United Future's support is somewhere in the range of 7.2% - 0.8%.
 
Competent political reporters know that poll interpretation is at its best when there are several points on the graph to be plotted, thus giving clear trends. Perhaps you should hire some competent people.
 
This is the second time this month your political staff have written unjustified speculation about United Future as if it were fact. I hope we are not seeing a trend developing.
 
Peter Dunne
Leader, United New Zealand Future
Parliament
